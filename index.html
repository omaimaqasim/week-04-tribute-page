Poet Of East

ALLAMA MUHAMMAD IQBAL

1877 – 1938

"Nations are born in the hearts of poets, they prosper and die in the hands of politicians."

BIOGRAPHY

Early Life

Allama Muhammad Iqbal was born on 9th November 1877 in Sialkot, Punjab (British India), into a Kashmiri family. His father, Sheikh Noor Muhammad, was a devout Muslim of humble means, and his mother, Imam Bibi, was a kind-hearted woman deeply respected for her piety.

Iqbal received his early education in Sialkot, where he studied Arabic and Persian. Later, he joined Government College Lahore, where he was influenced by Sir Thomas Arnold, a professor of philosophy, who greatly shaped his intellectual development.

Iqbal’s birthplace in Sialkot

Education

After Lahore, Iqbal went to Europe for higher studies. He studied at the University of Cambridge and then earned a doctorate in philosophy from the University of Munich, Germany. His exposure to Western philosophy enriched his thought while strengthening his vision for Islamic revival.

Cambridge University, where Iqbal studied

Poetry and Philosophy

Iqbal’s poetry, written in Urdu and Persian, inspired Muslims to rediscover their identity. Works such as Bang-e-Dra, Asrar-e-Khudi, and Payam-e-Mashriq reflect his ideas on selfhood, faith, and the awakening of nations.

Iqbal’s poetry inspired generations

Political Role

In his famous Allahabad Address of 1930, Iqbal envisioned a separate homeland for Muslims of India. His political philosophy guided the Pakistan Movement, earning him the title “Mufakkir-e-Pakistan” (Thinker of Pakistan).

Allahabad Address (1930)

Legacy

Iqbal passed away on 21st April 1938 in Lahore. Today, he is remembered as the “Poet of the East” and the “National Poet of Pakistan,” whose vision continues to inspire millions across the Muslim world.
