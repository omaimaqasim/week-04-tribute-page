Poet Of East

ALLAMA MUHAMMAD IQBAL

1877 – 1938

"Nations are born in the hearts of poets, they prosper and die in the hands of politicians."

BIOGRAPHY

Early Life

Allama Muhammad Iqbal (1877–1938), born in Sialkot, was a poet, philosopher, and visionary whose ideas inspired the Pakistan Movement. He is remembered as Shair-e-Mashriq (Poet of the East) and Mufakkir-e-Pakistan (Thinker of Pakistan).

Educated in Lahore, Cambridge, and Germany, Iqbal’s Urdu and Persian poetry awakened Muslims of the subcontinent to their identity. His Allahabad Address of 1930 laid the foundation for a separate homeland, and his works like Bang-e-Dra and Asrar-e-Khudi remain timeless sources of inspiration.
